Categories
Vehicle
Sub-Category Name What It Includes Budgeting/Tracking Purpose
Fuel / Gas Gasoline, Diesel, Electric vehicle (EV) charging costs. High-frequency, Variable Cost – For tracking your highest volume, day-to-day spending.
Maintenance Routine oil changes, filter replacements, fluid checks, minor tune-ups. Routine/Preventative Cost – To track essential upkeep and prevent future large repairs.
Repairs & Tires Purchase of new tire sets, unexpected major mechanical/body repairs (e.g., transmission, engine, large brake jobs). Infrequent, High-cost Variable – For planning an emergency repair fund.
Mandatory Fees Annual/Monthly Insurance premiums, Vehicle Registration renewal, mandatory inspection/license fees. Fixed/Annual Cost – To track predictable, required expenses, often paid in bulk.
Tolls & Parking Public parking meter fees, garage fees, highway/bridge tolls, parking apps. As-needed, Variable Cost – To track driving-related costs that are not fuel.
Upgrades & Accessories Window tinting/re-tinting, backup camera installation, multimedia system installation, dashcams, stereo systems, custom mats/covers, non-essential body kits. Discretionary, One-Time Cost – To track non-essential improvements and optional, long-lasting additions to the vehicle.
Fines & Penalties Parking tickets, speeding tickets, red light camera fines, late payment penalties. Non-essential, Avoidable Cost – Keeps negative/wasted spending separate from legitimate costs.
Cleaning Automated car washes, detailing services, car cleaning supplies. Discretionary Cost – To track optional aesthetic upkeep.
